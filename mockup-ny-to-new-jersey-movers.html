What To Know

Why Moving from NYC to New Jersey Requires the Right Movers

Many customers assume a NYC-to-NJ move is “basically local.” It isn’t — and the details matter.

NYC Building Rules Still Apply

Your current NYC building may require a COI, elevator reservation, loading dock coordination, and specific move-out hours. These requirements apply regardless of destination. We handle all of it.

New Jersey Delivery Access Varies

Hoboken and Jersey City have narrow streets and limited parking — and many buildings have their own COI and elevator requirements. Towns like Montclair and Ridgewood may have specific delivery restrictions. We plan in advance.

Short Distance Does Not Mean Simple

Crossing the Hudson means bridge or tunnel coordination, peak-hour timing, and separate pickup and delivery windows. Even a 10-mile move requires the same planning as a longer one.

Interstate Licensing Still Matters

Any move crossing state lines legally requires USDOT authority and FMCSA registration. Using an unlicensed mover for an NYC-to-NJ move is illegal. We’re fully licensed and insured.

Businesses Moving Across the Hudson

Office relocations from Manhattan, Long Island City, and Brooklyn to Jersey City, Hoboken, or Newark are common as NJ commercial space remains more affordable.

FAQ

NYC to New Jersey Moving — Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to move from NYC to New Jersey?

Most NYC-to-NJ moves cost between $800 and $4,500 depending on home size, access, distance, and packing needs. A studio or 1-bedroom to Hoboken or Jersey City typically costs $800–$1,500. A 2-bedroom to Bergen or Essex County typically costs $1,500–$2,800. Larger homes or distant destinations like Princeton or Morris County may cost $2,500–$4,500. All estimates are binding.

How long does a NYC to New Jersey move take?

Most NYC-to-NJ moves are completed in a single day. Hoboken, Jersey City, and nearby Hudson County destinations may be done in half a day. Moves to Morris County, Somerset County, or southern New Jersey typically take a full day and may require next-day delivery.

Do you move from all five NYC boroughs to New Jersey?

Yes. We serve Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island as pickup locations for moves to New Jersey. Each borough has its own access requirements — elevator bookings, COI needs, and loading dock coordination — and our team is experienced with all of them.

Do I need a Certificate of Insurance (COI) for my NYC building?

Many NYC buildings — particularly co-ops, condos, and managed rentals — require a COI from your moving company before allowing access on move day. Big Apple Moving provides building-compliant COIs as a standard part of our service.

Can you handle moves to Hoboken or Jersey City?

Absolutely. Hoboken and Jersey City are among our most common NJ destinations. We know these markets well — including narrow street access, building COI requirements, elevator reservations, and parking logistics.

Is a NYC to New Jersey move considered a long-distance move?

Legally, yes — any move that crosses state lines is classified as an interstate move under FMCSA regulations, regardless of distance. This means the mover must hold valid USDOT authority. Big Apple Moving is fully licensed for interstate moves, including the NYC-to-NJ corridor.

Do you offer packing services for NYC to NJ moves?

Yes. We offer full packing, partial packing (fragile items only), and unpacking at your NJ destination. Packing is quoted separately from the move itself.

Do you also move from New Jersey to NYC?

Yes. Big Apple Moving handles moves in both directions, including New Jersey to NYC relocations. We coordinate NYC building COI requirements, elevator reservations, parking, and delivery timing for a smooth move into the city.
