What We Carry — And What It Covers

Big Apple Moving carries multiple types of insurance. Here's what each covers so you know exactly how your belongings are protected on move day.

Cargo Insurance

Covers your belongings while in transit or in our care

General Liability

Covers property damage to your building or unit during the move

Workers' Compensation

Covers our crew — protects you from liability if a mover is injured

Valuation Options — Understanding Your Coverage Level

Released Value Protection

60¢ per pound, per item
  • Included at no extra charge
  • Federal minimum required coverage
  • Pays 60¢/lb regardless of item value — a 50lb TV gets $30 regardless of cost
  • Not adequate for high-value items

Full Value Protection

Replacement cost coverage
  • Lost or damaged items repaired, replaced, or cash settlement at current market value
  • Covers electronics, furniture, art, and valuables at actual value
  • Additional premium applies — ask for a quote
  • Recommended for moves with high-value items

Document the damage

As soon as you notice damage, photograph it clearly. Note the item, the nature of the damage, and when it was discovered.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our licensing, insurance, and coverage.

Is Big Apple Moving licensed in New York?
Yes. Big Apple Moving holds a New York State Department of Transportation (NYSDOT) household goods carrier authorization and is registered with the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) for interstate moves. We've maintained these licenses continuously since 1979. You can verify our FMCSA registration at safer.fmcsa.dot.gov by searching our company name.
What insurance does Big Apple Moving carry?
We carry three types of coverage: (1) Cargo insurance — covers your belongings while in our care and in transit. (2) General liability insurance — covers damage to your building, floors, walls, or property during the move. (3) Workers' compensation — covers our crew in the event of injury, protecting you from any secondary liability. All moves include Released Value Protection (60¢/lb) at no charge; Full Value Protection is available as an upgrade.
Can you provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) for my building?
Yes — and we do this regularly for NYC buildings. When you book, let us know your building's management company, any specific COI requirements (liability minimums, who to name as additional insured, etc.), and we'll prepare and send the certificate directly to building management. Most COIs are ready same-day. There is no extra charge for COI generation.
What is the difference between Released Value and Full Value Protection?
Released Value Protection is the federal minimum coverage included with every move at no extra charge. It pays 60 cents per pound per item — so a 50-pound TV would receive a $30 settlement regardless of its purchase price. Full Value Protection covers repair, replacement, or cash settlement at the item's current market value. For any move involving electronics, furniture, art, or items with significant monetary value, Full Value Protection is strongly recommended.
What do I do if something is damaged during my move?
First, photograph the damage clearly as soon as you notice it. Then contact our claims department in writing — by email or certified mail. Federal regulations require claims to be filed within 9 months of delivery for interstate moves; for local moves, contact us as promptly as possible. We'll acknowledge your claim within 30 days and work toward a resolution based on your valuation level.
Do you use subcontractors or your own movers?
Big Apple Moving uses its own moving teams and owned fleet. When you book with us, you know exactly who is showing up. We do not broker your move to a third-party company or use contract labor. Some moving brokers sell your job to whoever is available, which creates accountability gaps. With Big Apple Moving, the team you speak with is the team that moves you.
Are your movers background checked?
Yes. Every crew member at Big Apple Moving undergoes a background check before joining our team. They are also trained in-house on proper packing, furniture protection, building care (floor runners, elevator pads, corner protectors), and safe loading procedures. We've maintained these standards since 1979 — every mover represents our company, and we take that seriously.
